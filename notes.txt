1. There are over 260 known species of monkeys in the world.
2. Monkeys are divided into two main groups: Old World monkeys (found in Africa and Asia) and New World monkeys (found in the Americas).
3. Most monkeys have tails, which helps distinguish them from apes.
4. New World monkeys like spider monkeys have prehensile tails they can use to grip branches, almost like a fifth limb.
5. The mandrill is the world's largest monkey, with males weighing up to 35 kg (77 lbs).
6. The pygmy marmoset is the world's smallest monkey, weighing only about 100 grams and fitting in a human hand.
7. Monkeys are highly social animals and live in groups called troops, which can range from a few individuals to hundreds.
8. Monkeys communicate using vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language — some species have complex alarm calls for different predators.
9. Japanese macaques (snow monkeys) are known for bathing in hot springs during winter, one of the few non-human primates to do so.
10. Many monkey species are endangered due to habitat destruction, hunting, and the illegal pet trade.
